# Cybercrime Case Study - Phishing Attack

## Phishing
Phishing is one of the most widespread forms of cybercrime and remains highly effective despite widespread awareness campaigns.
The core tactic involves sending a message that appears to come from a trusted source - a company, an institution, or a known contact — in order to trick the recipient into handing over sensitive information such as passwords, banking details, or personal identification data.

## The Case Study
A realistic scenario targeting students goes as follows: a college student receives an email that looks like it has come from a well-known company, offering a paid internship position. The email has a professional layout, a convincing subject line along the lines of 'Urgent: Internship Offer — Response Required Within 12 Hours', and a link to a form asking for academic details and email login credentials under the pretence of account verification. The tight deadline is deliberate — it is designed to stop the recipient from pausing to think critically. The student, excited by the opportunity and under time pressure, fills in the form without checking whether the sender's email address is legitimate or whether the company's official website mentions any such programme. Within hours, they begin receiving notifications of unauthorised login attempts to their accounts.

## General Targets
Students and young professionals are frequent targets because they are actively searching for opportunities and are statistically more likely to respond quickly.
